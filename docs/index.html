The Dog Perspective
An open-source tale

Once upon a time, in a late nineteenth century Texas farm, in southern United States, there lived three old indomitable German bloodhounds and an old farmer went by the name of Mr. Lodestone who proudly owned them three, and who also owned his two sons, Jim and Fred and his four niggers and the wide ten-acre Texas farm which was known as the Lodestone Patch. The three dogs were called Brute, Shag and Tiger. Brute who happened to be my great-grandpa told this story to my grandpa who passed it on to my maw who told it to me before she died.


One day, Mr. Lodestone bought a new nigger, a young man with a good-natured face, and he bought him on the advice of his old friend Mr. Hopstone who lived in the nearby farm. And this new nigger was quite hardworking and Mr. Lodestone had no complaints at all about his niggers generally and his field was being plowed and the cotton was being brought in by the bale every day. Life was good in that old Texas farm. ...
